What are the evolutionary and socio-ecological factors that shape cultural differences in subjective well-being? My research examines how these factors influence the content of well-being (e.g., joy vs. meaning), its predictors (e.g., inner experience vs. social approval), and modes of pursuit (e.g., material vs. social). I combine perspectives from social psychology, behavioral ecology, and evolutionary theory to understand these dynamics across cultures. My work explores three core areas: (1) the socio-ecological and evolutionary origins of cultural variation in well-being; (2) the role of kinship intensity and ecological relatedness in shaping the relationship between personal freedom and happiness; and (3) the cultural psychology of honor, including how honor values shape social perception and compromise in interpersonal and political contexts.
